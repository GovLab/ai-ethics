The COVID-19 crisis has generated numerous initiatives where data and AI-based technologies are leveraged to inform decisions and manage uncertainties across the trajectory of health pandemics, as well as to accelerate the identification of interventions and treatments that may halt the spread of COVID-19. While many, but not all, deployments of AI may remain experimental and limited, it remains essential that we begin now to consider the ethical ramifications involved.

Historically, however, health and other emergencies have led to urgent and rapid uses of technology, without much attention to the wider social, economic, cultural, and political context. In addition, AI technologies pose unique threats to the privacy and security likely to exacerbate the already disparate effects of COVID-19 on vulnerable populations and marginalized communities. The urgency of a crisis may perpetuate habits, approaches, and norms that prove difficult to reverse. The use of data and AI in pandemic relief should thus include increasing literacy, awareness and education of the ethical implications of decisions made today on the use of data and AI.
